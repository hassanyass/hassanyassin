Melaka AI Hackathon

2nd Place Winner: Eat-In-Sight, an AI-powered dietary automation tool.

2nd Place Award
October 2025 Date

I was awarded Second Place at the Melaka AI Hackathon, a competitive, time-constrained innovation event focused on applying Artificial Intelligence to real-world problems. The award was achieved through the development of Eat-In-Sight, an AI-powered Telegram bot designed to promote healthier eating habits through intelligent automation and computer vision.

Eat-In-Sight enables users to simply upload an image of their meal via Telegram. The system analyzes the image using AI-based image understanding techniques to identify food components and estimate nutritional values, including macronutrients such as carbohydrates, proteins, and fats. Based on this analysis, the bot generates a structured meal breakdown that is easy for users to understand and act upon.

Beyond analysis, the solution focuses strongly on automation and long-term habit building. Each analyzed meal is automatically logged into a Notion calendar, allowing users to track their dietary patterns over time, review past meals, and reflect on their nutritional consistency. The automation pipeline was built using n8n, showcasing how AI systems can be seamlessly integrated into existing productivity tools to reduce friction and manual effort.

This project demonstrated how computer vision, workflow automation, and user-centric AI design can work together to transform everyday health decisions into data-driven insights.
